Selected Work

A selection of analytics, governance, and decision-support projects. Each reflects analytical flexibility, structured problem solving, and a focus on responsible impact.

Hockey Analytics System – KPI Matrix & ML Modeling (In Progress)

In-progress analytics framework developed for a coach, combining structured KPI design with exploratory machine learning to support real-time decision-making and long-term player development. Designing a SQL-backed data pipeline, Power BI dashboards, and R/Python models with an emphasis on responsible evaluation and avoiding misleading metrics.

AI & Student Engagement Study – Behavioural Analytics & Policy

Designed and analyzed a survey on how students use AI for academic work, focusing on engagement, decision-making, and problem-solving behaviour. Interpreted usage trends, visualized patterns, and examined mismatches between time spent and expected output quality. Findings informed governance recommendations for faculty, contributing to Saint Mary’s academic AI policy.

RBC Hackathon – Lumin Mobility Prototype

Led the design of “Lumin,” a human-centered mobility prototype built with Virtusa and Microsoft tools to improve transportation access and community connectivity in Halifax. Defined user flows, system logic, and feature relationships, envisioning employer-sponsored transit, business-to-business support, and event coordination. Delivered a clickable demo focused on connecting people, opportunities, and sustainable transport options.

Public Safety & Water Risk Education – National Drowning Prevention Week

Led public-facing initiatives for National Drowning Prevention Week, focused on educating the public about behaviours, factors, and warning signs that create unsafe aquatic environments. Designed surveys, social media campaigns, and on-site outreach to debunk common misconceptions and promote safer decisions. This work grounds my analytics interests in governance, risk communication, and how people actually behave around safety.
