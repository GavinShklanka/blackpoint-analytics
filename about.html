About Me

A background shaped by psychology, public safety, and applied research — now focused on analytics, problem solving, and building responsible, human-centered systems.

Psychology foundation. Technical growth. Human-centered analytics.

Before analytics, I studied psychology — not just theory, but the research and technical side of it: gathering data, examining behaviour, understanding motivation, and analyzing how people think and make decisions. That foundation taught me to look past surface-level answers, break down problems, and understand the human factors behind every outcome.

Over time, I became increasingly drawn to the analytical techniques behind research: structuring information, identifying patterns, and interpreting what data actually means. That interest grew as I worked in public safety, where risk communication and human behaviour directly shape real-world decisions. These experiences naturally led me toward analytics — a field that blends structured thinking, technology, and the behavioural insights I’ve always cared about.

Now, as a Master of Business Data Analytics student at Saint Mary’s University, I’m expanding that foundation into technical depth: machine learning, data engineering, visualization, and responsible system design. What motivates me is not just solving problems, but understanding them — and building analytical solutions that are clear, safe, and aligned with human behaviour.

I’m early in this career, but I bring something important: an analytical mindset shaped by psychology, a humanistic lens, and a strong interest in how technology can be applied thoughtfully to help people, improve systems, and support better decision-making.
