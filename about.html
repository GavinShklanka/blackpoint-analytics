About Me

A background shaped by research discipline and operational work—now focused on analytics, problem-solving, and building data-driven insight for meaningful decisions.

Research foundation. Operational experience. Business-focused analytics.

My academic foundation began in psychology and applied research, where I developed strong skills in structuring information, analyzing data, evaluating patterns, and communicating results clearly. That training taught me to work systematically, break down complex questions, and develop evidence-based conclusions.

I expanded that foundation in public safety and operational roles where decisions needed to be timely, structured, and grounded in reliable information. This experience strengthened my ability to assess situations, align teams, and translate observations into actionable recommendations. Those environments showed me the importance of clear data, good processes, and dependable systems.

Today, as a Master of Business Data Analytics student, I apply that background to technical work—building KPI frameworks, SQL pipelines, analytical models, dashboards, and decision-support tools that help organizations understand performance and operate effectively. My interests focus on the point where data, operations, and governance meet.

I’m early in my analytics career, but bring a combination of structured thinking, technical capability, and operational awareness. I care about producing analysis that is accurate, clear, and genuinely useful—supporting better decisions, stronger processes, and responsible analytical practice.
