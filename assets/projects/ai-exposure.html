AI Exposure Research

An analytical study on how students use AI academically, focusing on workload patterns, risk indicators, and planning considerations.

Overview

This project analyzes AI-use patterns across different academic tasks to understand how students distribute work, where reliance forms, and what factors contribute to academic integrity risk. The analysis supports data-informed decisions around guidance, monitoring, and instructional design.

Key Analytical Areas

  • Usage Segmentation: Identification of assistive, replacement, and dependency-driven use patterns.
  • Workload Distribution: How students allocate time and effort when integrating AI tools into coursework.
  • Risk Indicators: Behaviours associated with over-reliance, task misunderstanding, or academic misalignment.
  • Recommendations: Insight for planning academic support, tool guidance, and integrity-aligned policies.
